How to Play

Running a Case

  1. Read the Assistance Unit. Each case opens with a household summary — members, ages, income, and non-financial flags (elderly, disabled, veteran, immigration status, etc.). These drive the determination.
  2. Work the determination steps. Answer each prompt the way a caseworker would: compute net monthly income (deductions applied in the correct federal order), decide eligibility, and calculate the benefit amount — or, in later modules, judge ABAWD time-limit and noncitizen status.
  3. Get graded instantly. Every answer returns a pass/validation result with a plain-language explanation and the exact policy citation (ACL, CFR, FNS COLA) it comes from.

Two ways to play: 📘 Learning Mode — a coach walks you through the method on every step, all modules are open, and you can retry; nothing is scored. 🎯 Graded Shift — timed and scored, with the leaderboard and the Alaska Exemption in play.

Modules unlock as you go

Six modules run from basics to a capstone: SNAP Basics → Deductions → Elderly & Disabled → ABAWD Work Requirements → Noncitizen Eligibility → Capstone. Clear 3 cases in a module to unlock the next.

Scoring — accuracy is king

Two quality measures: Accuracy = share of steps right. Payment Error Rate = share of benefit dollars mis-issued (QC-style, small errors excluded) — a big miscalculation hurts far more than a tiny one. Above a 6% PER, an H.R.1-style cost-share penalty cuts your score. Speed and volume factor in too. Submit a shift to post it to the Leaderboard.
